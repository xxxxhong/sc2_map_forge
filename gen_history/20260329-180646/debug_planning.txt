# StarCraft II Scenario: "The Proxy Gambit"

## Scenario Overview
**Theme:** 借刀杀人 (Kill with a Borrowed Knife) – The player must manipulate enemy forces into destroying each other while preserving their own limited resources.

**Map Context:** The battle takes place on the uploaded terrain (20260305-105136.SC2Map), which features a narrow central chokepoint with elevated platforms on both sides and destructible rocks separating two enemy encampments.

---

## Force Composition

### Player Forces (Terran) – 3 Units
1. **Ghost** (Cloaked) – Primary operative
   - Equipment: Snipe, EMP, Cloak
   - Role: Reconnaissance and triggering enemy conflict

2. **Raven** – Support unit
   - Equipment: Auto-Turret, Interference Matrix
   - Role: Area denial and tech disruption

3. **SCV** – Utility unit
   - Equipment: Repair, Construct Bunker
   - Role: Constructing defensive structures if needed

### Enemy Forces (Protoss) – 5 Units (Two Factions)
**Faction A – Zealot Patrol** (3 units)
- 2x Zealot
- 1x Sentry (with Guardian Shield, Force Field)

**Faction B – Stalker Team** (2 units)
- 2x Stalker (with Blink)

**Note:** These two Protoss factions are hostile to each other due to a lore-based schism (Tal'darim vs Daelaam).

---

## Positioning & Phases

### Initial Setup:
- **Player Start:** Southern high ground overlooking central chokepoint
- **Faction A (Zealots):** West side of central area, behind destructible rocks
- **Faction B (Stalkers):** East side, patrolling near a power field
- **Neutral Threat:** 2x Elite Zerg Roaches in a pit between factions (activated by player action)

### Phase 1: Reconnaissance
- Ghost scouts enemy positions while cloaked
- Raven provides aerial surveillance
- Player identifies:
  - Destructible rocks separating factions
  - Power conduits that can be sabotaged
  - Patrol patterns creating temporary vulnerabilities

### Phase 2: Provocation (借刀杀人 Execution)
**Primary Stratagem Options:**
1. **Snipe the Sentry** from cloak, making Zealots charge toward last known position (which is near Stalkers)
2. **EMP the Stalkers** while they're near Zealot patrol path, causing them to attack nearby units
3. **Use SCV to repair** a neutral structure that, when activated, draws both factions toward it
4. **Drop Auto-Turret** between factions to draw fire that hits enemy units

### Phase 3: Exploitation
- Once factions are engaged, player uses:
  - Ghost to snipe weakened survivors
  - Raven to disable key abilities
  - SCV to claim resources from fallen enemies

### Phase 4: Cleanup
- Eliminate remaining wounded units
- Secure the central area

---

## Victory/Defeat Conditions

### Victory:
- Both Protoss factions eliminated
- At least 2 player units survive
- Central control point secured for 60 seconds

### Defeat:
- All player units destroyed
- One Protoss faction remains at full strength (3+ units)
- Player directly attacks first (breaking "borrowed knife" principle)

### Bonus Objectives:
- **Perfect Stratagem:** Enemy factions eliminate each other completely without player direct damage
- **Zero Casualties:** All player units survive
- **Efficient Execution:** Complete within 5 minutes

---

## Terrain Utilization Notes

### Key Features from Map:
1. **Central Chokepoint:**
   - Natural funnel for forcing enemy engagements
   - High ground advantage for player observation

2. **Destructible Rocks:**
   - Separate the two Protoss factions initially
   - Can be destroyed to create sudden contact
   - Player can bait enemies into destroying them

3. **Elevated Platforms:**
   - Provide sightlines for Ghost snipe
   - Safe positioning for Raven
   - SCV can construct temporary bunker here

4. **Power Conduits:**
   - Can be sabotaged to create explosions damaging nearby units
   - Distraction mechanism

5. **Narrow Pathways:**
   - Limit unit movement, creating predictable engagement zones
   - Force Field from Sentry can trap units

### Strategic Applications:
- **Lure Tactics:** Draw one faction through destructible terrain toward the other
- **Bait Positioning:** Place Auto-Turrets where enemy AoE will hit opposing faction
- **Timed Engagements:** Use patrol patterns to ensure factions meet when both are present
- **Environmental Kills:** Sabotage terrain to damage both factions simultaneously

## Design Notes for Implementation

1. **AI Behavior:**
   - Factions prioritize attacking whatever damages them first
   - Aggro ranges extended slightly to ensure chain reactions
   - Faction A is melee-focused, Faction B is ranged

2. **Trigger Suggestions:**
   - When faction A takes damage from faction B, set permanent hostility
   - Destructible rocks destruction triggers both factions to investigate
   - Player unit survival tracked for bonus objectives

3. **Balance Considerations:**
   - Ghost cloak energy drains faster than normal to prevent passive play
   - Raven has limited energy (2 Auto-Turrets max)
   - Protoss units have slightly reduced HP to ensure mutual destruction

This scenario emphasizes indirect warfare, positioning, and timing over direct combat, perfectly embodying the "借刀杀人" stratagem within StarCraft II's mechanics.
