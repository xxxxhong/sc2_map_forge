Below is a structured **StarCraft II battle scenario plan** built around the stratagem **“借刀杀人”** (“Kill with a borrowed knife” / manipulate others into destroying your enemy for you), using the provided terrain map **`sota.SC2Map`** as the battlefield basis.

---

# Scenario Title
**Borrowed Blades**

# Core Stratagem
**借刀杀人**  
The Terran player is too weak to defeat the Zerg head-on. Instead, they must exploit the battlefield and provoke hostile forces/environmental defenses into damaging or destroying the Zerg for them, then strike at the right moment.

This is represented through:
- Terran using bait, positioning, and tactical withdrawal
- Zerg overcommitting into a dangerous area
- Terrain or neutral defensive elements acting as the “borrowed knife”
- Terran finishing the weakened enemy with a compact strike force

---

# 1. Scenario Overview

## Narrative Premise
A small Terran recon-detachment has been trapped near an abandoned fortified sector on **sota.SC2Map**. A Zerg raiding pack is hunting them. The Terrans cannot win a direct engagement, but they discover that the old defensive zone and local battlefield hazards can be used against the Zerg. By luring the swarm into a kill zone, the Terrans can let the environment or neutral automated defenses tear into the Zerg before committing their own force.

## Intended Gameplay
- Small-scale tactical skirmish
- Emphasis on movement, baiting, and timing
- Player should avoid direct early confrontation
- Success comes from **drawing the Zerg into crossfire/chokepoints/hazard zones**

# 2. Force Composition

Unit counts are kept around the requested **10 vs 10**.

## Player Force: Terran
Recommended composition: **10 units**

1. **1x Reaper**
   - Primary bait/scout unit
   - Used to provoke enemy aggro and retreat safely over terrain

2. **2x Marines**
   - Basic ranged support
   - Hold narrow approaches and finish weakened targets

3. **2x Marauders**
   - Anchor frontline
   - Good versus tougher Zerg units and useful in choke fighting

4. **2x Hellions**
   - Harassment and kiting tools
   - Excellent for leading Zerg into a trap and softening light targets

5. **1x Siege Tank**
   - Main punishment unit
   - Deployed at the kill zone to capitalize once Zerg are funneled

6. **1x Medivac**
   - Sustains the small force
   - Allows tactical repositioning or emergency extraction

7. **1x Widow Mine**
   - Hidden trigger element in the trap
   - Helps sell the idea of layered, indirect killing

### Terran Design Intent
This force is not meant to overpower the Zerg in open ground. It is built around:
- Mobility
- Baiting
- Chokepoint control
- Bursting weakened enemies after trap activation

---

## Enemy Force: Zerg
Recommended composition: **10 units**

1. **1x Roach**
   - Durable frontliner
   - Leads the push and survives long enough to reach the trap area

2. **1x Ravager**
   - Adds pressure with ranged siege threat
   - Forces Terran not to camp too openly

3. **4x Zerglings**
   - Fast pursuit units
   - Most likely to chase bait and rush into danger first

4. **2x Hydralisks**
   - Main damage dealers
   - Dangerous if preserved, so they should be key trap targets

5. **1x Baneling**
   - High-threat punish unit against clustered Terran infantry
   - Encourages player to use spacing and indirect kills

6. **1x Queen**
   - Slight sustain/synaptic anchor presence
   - Can represent the central command organism of the raiding party

### Zerg Design Intent
The Zerg force should feel:
- Aggressive
- Pursuit-oriented
- Strong in a direct skirmish
- Vulnerable when compressed into terrain hazards and artillery zones

# 3. Terrain Utilization Concept

Because the exact XML layout is tied to **`sota.SC2Map`**, this scenario assumes the map includes some combination of:
- Narrow access lanes or ramps
- Elevated terrain
- A ruined installation, outpost, or fortified pocket
- Open ground where Zerg can mass, contrasted with a tight kill zone

## Key Battlefield Areas
Define 4 practical zones on the map:

### A. Terran Forward Contact Zone
- Mid-map or slightly Terran-side open terrain
- Where the Reaper/Hellions first spot and bait the Zerg
- Should be open enough that the player can safely retreat

### B. Funnel Approach
- A ramp, canyon, wreck corridor, bridge, or narrow pass
- Critical for the stratagem
- Limits Zerg surround potential and compresses their formation

### C. Borrowed Knife Zone
This is the core of the scenario. It should be one of the following depending on what the terrain supports:

**Preferred options:**
- An abandoned defense platform with neutral auto-turrets
- A line-of-fire corridor overlooked by high ground emplacements
- A destructible debris lane that channels enemy movement into Tank fire
- A hazardous area such as fire vents, unstable ground, or explosive objects

This zone represents the “knife” not originally belonging to the Terran force.

### D. Terran Ambush Position
- Behind or beside the kill zone
- Siege Tank, Marines, and Marauders establish here
- Medivac supports from the rear
- Widow Mine is buried near the approach or center of the funnel

# 4. Scenario Flow and Phases

## Phase 1: Contact and Recognition
**Objective:** Discover the enemy and identify the trap opportunity.

- The Terran player begins with units split:
  - Reaper and Hellions forward
  - Main force near a defensive fallback point
- Zerg are initially patrolling or advancing from their side of the map
- The player should quickly realize that direct engagement is unfavorable

### Gameplay Notes
- Reaper gains vision of the Zerg pack
- Optional dialogue/intel hint:
  - “Those ruins still have active defense signatures.”
  - “Don’t fight them here. Pull them into the old perimeter.”

### Tactical Purpose
This phase teaches the player:
- The Zerg are stronger in a straight fight
- Retreat is not failure; it is the strategy

---

## Phase 2: The Lure
**Objective:** Draw the Zerg into pursuit.

- Reaper/Hellions poke the Zerg, dealing minor damage
- Zerg aggro activates and they begin chasing
- The player must withdraw toward the funnel approach

### Important Design Behavior
- Zerglings and Baneling should surge ahead
- Roach/Ravager/Queen/Hydralisks trail slightly behind
- This creates natural stagger and makes the trap visually satisfying

### Failure Risk
- If player overcommits in open terrain, Hydralisks and Ravager can punish them
- If the player retreats too slowly, Zerglings may surround the forward units

---

## Phase 3: Borrowed Knife Trigger
**Objective:** Force the Zerg into external damage.

Once the Zerg enter the designated kill zone:
- Neutral turrets activate against the nearest biological hostiles
- Or environmental hazards erupt
- Or line-of-fire from elevated emplacements begins striking the clustered Zerg
- Widow Mine triggers on the vanguard
- Siege Tank begins shelling the compressed formation

### Best Expected Target Order
1. Zerglings
2. Baneling
3. Hydralisks
4. Ravager / Queen
5. Roach last

### Visual and Tactical Outcome
The player should see:
- Frontline Zerg evaporate or scatter
- The enemy formation lose cohesion
- The dangerous ranged core become exposed

This is the heart of **借刀杀人**:
- The Terrans are not winning with their own blade alone
- They weaponize the battlefield and enemy momentum

---

## Phase 4: Counterstrike
**Objective:** Clean up surviving Zerg.

After trap activation:
- Terran Marines and Marauders step forward
- Tank remains sieged if lane control remains safe
- Medivac stabilizes any wounded frontline units
- Hellions circle or chase weakened Hydralisks/Queen
- Reaper can jump behind to finish damaged units or deny retreat

### Tactical Shift
This phase transitions from:
- Avoidance and manipulation
to
- Decisive local superiority

The player should feel rewarded for patience and setup.

---

## Phase 5: Final Resolution
**Objective:** Eliminate the remaining Zerg command presence.

Usually the last survivors should be one or more of:
- Roach
- Queen
- Ravager

These units are sturdy enough to create a brief final exchange, but not enough to reverse the battle if the trap worked correctly.

# 5. Positioning Plan

## Terran Initial Positioning
- **Reaper + 2 Hellions**: Forward reconnaissance route near central lanes
- **Siege Tank + 2 Marines + 2 Marauders + Medivac**: Behind the choke, near the ambush line
- **Widow Mine**: Burrowed at the mouth of the funnel or just inside the kill zone

## Zerg Initial Positioning
- Entire Zerg force begins on opposite side of the map in a loose advancing pack
- Suggested front-to-back order:
  - Zerglings
  - Baneling
  - Roach
  - Ravager
  - Hydralisks
  - Queen

This arrangement naturally causes:
- Fast units to enter first
- Backline units to step into danger after the chase begins

# 6. Victory and Defeat Conditions

## Primary Victory Condition
- **Destroy all Zerg combat units**

## Secondary/Optional Victory Conditions
These reinforce the stratagem:
1. **Trigger the kill zone before losing more than 2 Terran units**
2. **Kill at least 4 Zerg units using neutral defenses or environmental hazards**
3. **Keep the Siege Tank alive**
4. **Preserve the Reaper** as the designated lure operative

## Defeat Conditions
1. **All Terran units destroyed**
2. **Siege Tank destroyed before the kill zone is triggered**
   - Optional if you want a stricter puzzle feel
3. **More than 5 Terran units lost before Phase 4**
   - Represents failure to execute the stratagem properly
4. **Zerg breach the Terran fallback zone**
   - If using a defendable bunker/outpost endpoint

# 7. Terrain Utilization Notes

Since the map file is provided externally, below are implementation notes tied to common SC2 terrain logic.

## Chokepoints
- Use any narrow pass, bridge, or ramp as the main funnel
- Ideal width should limit a full Zerg surround
- Ensure Hydralisks cannot all fire at once until they step forward

## High Ground
- Put the Siege Tank and Marines on elevated ground if available
- This reinforces the sense of a prepared ambush
- Reaper can exploit cliff paths to maintain bait pressure without being trapped

## Neutral Defenses / Borrowed Knife Elements
Recommended implementations:
- **2 neutral missile/auto-turret structures** covering a corridor
- **Line of explosive barrels** near choke entrance
- **Periodic flame vents** or **collapsible debris** in pursuit lane
- **Watchtower or sensor zone** revealing enemy movement before trap activation

The neutral danger should be strong enough to matter, but not so strong that it wins automatically without Terran support.

## Sight Lines
- Use blockers and corners so the player cannot simply attack from max range immediately
- The trap should require enemy commitment into the zone
- Vision from Reaper or watchtower can help prepare timing

## Retreat Paths
- The lure route must be readable and survivable
- Reaper jump ledges and Hellion maneuver space are valuable
- Avoid dead-end retreat routes unless the scenario is intentionally puzzle-hard

## Destructibles
If the map contains destructible rocks/debris:
- Use them to create alternate routes that are initially closed
- Optional interaction:
  - Terran can break side debris before battle to open a safe firing lane
  - Zerg default path remains through the dangerous central zone

# 8. Suggested Trigger/Encounter Logic

## Trigger Set A: Aggro
- When Terran forward unit enters detection radius or attacks Zerg:
  - Zerg pack becomes hostile
  - Patrol switches to pursuit

## Trigger Set B: Trap Activation
- When 4+ Zerg units enter the kill zone:
  - Neutral defenses awaken / hazards activate
  - Audio cue: “Perimeter grid online.”
  - Siege Tank AI hint or objective update appears

## Trigger Set C: Counterattack Prompt
- When Zerg lose 30–40% of their force in the trap:
  - Objective updates: “Enemy formation disrupted. Engage now.”

## Trigger Set D: Fail State
- If Terran engages in open field and loses key units too early:
  - The battle becomes much harder but not instantly impossible
  - Optional difficulty tuning

---

# 9. Difficulty Tuning Notes

## Easier Version
- Remove Ravager
- Replace with 1 extra Zergling or Roach
- Make neutral defenses stronger
- Give Terran one bunker or more Marine support

## Harder Version
- Add a second Baneling or second Roach
- Reduce neutral defense damage
- Force the player to manually activate the trap
- Place Hydralisks farther back so they survive the first blast unless the lure is perfect

---

# 10. Why This Fits “借刀杀人”

This scenario embodies the stratagem because:
- The Terran player is not expected to overpower the Zerg directly
- They must manipulate enemy aggression
- The real killing blow comes from **borrowed force**:
  - neutral defenses,
  - preexisting battlefield hazards,
  - terrain funneling,
  - and enemy overextension
- The player’s role is to engineer the conditions for destruction rather than brute-force the win

# 11. Compact Scenario Summary

## Terran
- Reaper, Marines, Marauders, Hellions, Siege Tank, Medivac, Widow Mine
- Small, tactical, trap-oriented force

## Zerg
- Zerglings, Baneling, Roach, Ravager, Hydralisks, Queen
- Aggressive pursuit force stronger in direct combat

## Flow
1. Scout Zerg
2. Bait with Reaper/Hellions
3. Retreat through choke
4. Trigger neutral defenses/hazards
5. Siege Tank and hidden mine punish clustered Zerg
6. Terran counterpush to finish survivors

## Win Condition
- Destroy all Zerg

## Lose Condition
- Terran wiped out, or trap fails and key units are lost too early
