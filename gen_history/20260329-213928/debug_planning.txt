# StarCraft II Scenario: "The Distant Roar"

## Scenario Overview
**Stratagem:** 调虎离山 (Lure the Tiger from the Mountain)  
**Theme:** A small Protoss reconnaissance force must lure the main Zerg defenders away from a critical objective, allowing a secondary force to strike.

## Force Composition

### Player (Protoss) - 10 Units
- **Main Lure Force (6 units):**
  - 2 Stalkers (mobile, can blink)
  - 2 Adepts (shade ability for positioning)
  - 1 Sentry (force field/guardian shield)
  - 1 Oracle (revelation, mobility)
- **Secondary Strike Force (4 units):**
  - 2 Zealots (charge ability)
  - 1 Dark Templar (permanent cloaking)
  - 1 High Templar (psi storm)

### Enemy (Zerg) - 10 Units
- **Primary Defense (6 units):**
  - 2 Roaches (tanky, ranged)
  - 2 Hydralisks (anti-air, ranged)
  - 1 Queen (transfusion, creep spread)
  - 1 Ravager (corrosive bile)
- **Objective Guard (4 units):**
  - 2 Zerglings (fast, melee)
  - 1 Baneling (explosive)
  - 1 Spore Crawler (static defense)

## Terrain Utilization (Based on sota.SC2Map)
**Key Terrain Features:**
- **Central Choke Point:** Narrow pass with high ground on both sides
- **Eastern Plateau:** Elevated position overlooking objective
- **Western Ravine:** Concealed approach path
- **Objective Zone:** Small clearing with mineral-like crystals (target)

## Phases & Positioning

### Phase 1: The Lure (0:00-1:30)
**Player Positioning:**
- Lure Force (6 units): Western edge of central choke
- Strike Force (4 units): Hidden in ravine (invisible due to terrain)

**Enemy Positioning:**
- Primary Defense (6 units): Patrolling central area
- Objective Guard (4 units): Stationed at objective zone

**Action:** Player uses Oracle to reveal enemy positions. Stalkers/Adepts engage Zerg defense force from range, then retreat through choke point. Sentry places force field to split enemy forces.

### Phase 2: The Chase (1:30-3:00)
**Player Action:** Lure Force continues retreating, using blink and shade abilities to maintain distance while taking potshots. Goal: Draw all 6 primary defenders westward away from objective.

**Enemy Response:** Zerg forces pursue aggressively. Queen uses transfusion on damaged units. Ravager attempts corrosive bile on choke points.

### Phase 3: The Strike (3:00-4:00)
**Player Action:** Once primary defenders are sufficiently distant (minimum 2 screens away), Strike Force emerges from ravine. Dark Templar eliminates spore crawler. Zealots charge zerglings. High Templar uses psi storm on clustered defenders.

**Enemy Response:** Objective Guard defends automatically. Primary defenders may attempt to return if player fails to maintain engagement.

## Victory/Defeat Conditions

### Victory:
- **Primary:** Destroy all 4 Objective Guard units within 60 seconds of engagement
- **Secondary:** Lose no more than 3 units from Strike Force
- **Bonus:** Keep all Lure Force units alive

### Defeat:
- Lose all Strike Force units
- Fail to destroy Objective Guard within time limit
- Lose more than 5 total units

## Terrain-Specific Notes
1. **High Ground Advantage:** Use eastern plateau for initial Oracle scouting
2. **Choke Points:** Force fields in central choke can delay returning Zerg
3. **Concealment:** Ravine provides visual cover until units emerge
4. **Pathing:** Zerglings fastest through narrow paths; position force fields accordingly
5. **Elevation:** Stalkers on high ground have extended vision range

## Player Tips
1. **Micro Management:** Use blink to dodge ravager biles; shade to block paths
2. **Ability Timing:** Save psi storm for when zerglings cluster
3. **Vision Control:** Keep Oracle alive for revelation of returning forces
4. **Engagement Discipline:** Lure Force should damage but not overcommit

## Scenario Balance Notes
- Zerg units have 15% increased health to compensate for player micro advantage
- Objective zone has limited entry points favoring defenders
- Time pressure prevents turtling strategies
- Player success depends on spatial awareness and multitasking

This scenario emphasizes the classic "lure and strike" tactic, requiring players to master unit preservation while executing a timed assault. The terrain naturally supports the stratagem with clear separation between lure and strike paths.
